Abstract

This paper presents a corpus of Livvi-Karelian, which is currently considered a low-resource language. The primary objective of this research is the initial collection and annotation of speech data, along with the development of a transcription dictionary. The presented corpus consists of transcripts of audio recordings from 17 speakers, comprising 7 males and 10 females, all sourced from radio broadcasts. The transcribed and segmented transcripts represent audio data of more than 5 hours. The corpus contains 32,466 word occurrences, with the total vocabulary of 10K unique words. This corpus also includes cases of code-switching between Livvi-Karelian and Russian, thus being a source for in-depth analysis of language dynamics and bilingualism. The collected data can be employed for training and testing an automatic speech recognition system for the Livvi-Karelian dialect of the Karelian language. Additionally, the corpus will be further expanded to encompass the phenomenon of code-switching in the modern Karelian language.

AnKaS Database Key Information

Database of Annotations of Karelian Speech (AnKaS) includes timestamps, textual transcriptions, and code-switching marks of Livvi-Karelian radio broadcasts.

The database is represented in JSON format. A separate .json file was created for each speaker. The following keys are used:

  • "phrase_id" is the phrase number for this speaker;
  • "link" is a link to the audio recording;
  • "time_start" is start time of the phrase;
  • "time_end" is end time of the phrase;
  • "sentence" is textual transcription;
  • "sentence_rus" is textual transcription with code-switching, indicated with brackets and the tag "rus";

Vocabulary Information

voc.txt contains list of words from textual transcriptions from AnKaS database with their phonemic representation. Vocabulary includes Karelian words as well as most frequent Russian words. Transcriptions for Russian words were made according to Russian transcribing rules.
